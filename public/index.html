The initial idea

Your own agent, living in WhatsApp, whose only job is getting you a job.

It started with an open source project that caught fire: an agentic framework that reads job postings, scores them against your background, tailors your CV, drafts the cover letter and preps you for the interview. Tens of thousands of people starred it in weeks. Its author used it to send 69 applications and land an offer.

  • The whole application loop is agentic — evaluate, tailor, draft, prep, track
  • The demand is not theoretical — tens of thousands of stars, thousands of forks, in weeks
  • But it lives on a laptop — Python, LaTeX, a terminal and a Claude Code subscription

The big idea

The Caribbean loses its people and imports its workers.

Brain drain and labour shortage at once — because a worker on one island can't see the job one island over. A coordination failure, and those are solvable.

  • Locals considered first — agents that catch ghost postings
  • A portable skills passport — CARICOM free movement, made real
  • Live labour intelligence — for governments steering on 2 surveys/year
